How Did Tom Brady Become the Biggest “Steal” in NFL Draft History?

Due to his late selection, Brady is regarded as the biggest “steal” in the history of the NFL Draft. Starting in his second year, he set an NFL record by remaining with the Patriots for a total of 20 seasons as the team’s quarterback. In his first year as a starting quarterback, he won the Super Bowl, making him one of only two quarterbacks to do it. He never had a losing season as a starting quarterback for the Patriots, and he is the NFL’s all-time leader in victories at quarterback.

He is the most successful quarterback in NFL history, having guided the Patriots to 16 division championships. He has the best postseason record in NFL history, 30-11, and has made more playoff appearances than any other player. In the years since 2011, Brady has guided the Patriots to an NFL-record eight consecutive AFC title games. Brady has also set a new NFL record by being named to 14 Pro Bowls.

For his apparent role in the Deflategate football tampering scandal, Brady was suspended for the first four games of the 2016 season. Two of the next three Super Bowls were won by Brady and the Patriots.

Will Tom Brady’s Commentary Career Impact His Legacy as an NFL Legend?
