George Santos: According to his campaign’s online bio, which has since been changed, Mr. Santos is a first-generation American who was born in the borough of Queens in New York City.

The website said that his grandparents “ran away from Jewish persecution in Ukraine, settled in Belgium, and then ran away from persecution again during World War II” before moving to Brazil. His parents were born in Brazil, but they eventually moved to the United States “in search of the American dream.”

Mr. Santos also said that he went to New York City’s Baruch College before becoming a “seasoned Wall Street financier and investor” who had worked at prestigious companies like Goldman Sachs and Citigroup.

Mr. Santos also said that he “worked hard” to get 13 properties and that his mother was “in her office in the South Tower” of the World Trade Center on September 11. His campaign’s website said as recently as October that she had lived through the attack but died a few years later.

With his victory in November, Mr. Santos became the first openly gay Republican to win a seat in the House of Representatives as a non-incumbent. He is still fairly new to politics in the United States.

The Daily Beast says that Santos, who says he has “never experienced discrimination in the Republican Party,” broke new ground this year when he became the first openly gay non-incumbent GOP candidate to be elected to Congress.

But according to court records that the Daily Beast found, Santos seems to be going through a divorce with a woman in Queens County, New York, in September 2019. The divorce, which Santos hasn’t talked about in public, makes his political and biographical claims even less sure.

“I am openly gay and have never had a problem with my sexual identity in the last ten years,” Santos told USA Today in October in response to criticism about his support for Florida’s “don’t say gay” law, which was signed into law this year by Republican Gov. Ron DeSantis.
