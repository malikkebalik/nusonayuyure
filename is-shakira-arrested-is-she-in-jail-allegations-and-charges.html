After years of wrangling with Spanish authorities, a Barcelona court determined that Shakira will be put on trial for suspected tax fraud.

Shakira has been ordered to spend more than eight years in jail and pay a fine of more than $24 million by Spanish prosecutors as they continue their years-long case against the Colombian pop diva.

Shakira has always refuted the charges. Shakira’s managers said in a statement that the 14.5 million euros “they were claiming” had been settled and that she “has no tax debts with the Spanish Treasury.”

Although no date has been set, a court has approved a trial in a town near to Barcelona. Her attorneys intend to provide a statement of defense in the next weeks.

Is Shakira Currently Arrested?

Suspicion of tax fraud gained widespread attention when a Spanish court brought accusations against the Colombian star and Hips Don’t Lie singer. Shakira is neither incarcerated nor doing time in jail right now.

The trial for Shakira has not yet been set for a date. Shakira may serve up to eight years in jail after being ordered by the court to face trial for suspected tax fraud.

The Colombian singer, who is accused of neglecting to pay taxes in Spain between 2012 and 2014, is being prosecuted after six authorities persuaded a Spanish court to order that action be taken.

According to Shakira’s representatives, the allegations are “a total breach of her rights,” and that she has “always cooperated and abided by the law, showing flawless behavior as an individual and taxpayer.”

Prosecutors have said they will seek a €24 million fine in addition to a prison term if the artist is proven guilty. A spokesperson for Shakira previously said: “The singer is confident of her innocence and, as a result, does not accept compensation.”

What allegations and accusations have been leveled against Shakira?

Shakira is being charged by the Spanish authorities with tax evasion, and she might serve up to eight years in prison. Shakira was one of several well-known persons listed in the so-called “Pandor Papers” financial record leak.

She was accused of setting up front companies in tax havens to conceal the scope of her wealth.

Prosecutors believe Shakira stayed in Spain for a longer amount of time than was required to be deemed a resident, despite the fact that she maintains she was there for less than 183 days during the relevant period, exempting her from resident status and the associated taxes.

Shakira gave a witness statement in front of a court in 2019 as part of an inquiry into her suspected tax cheating. She adamantly denied doing anything wrong.

On September 27, 2022, a Spanish court in Esplugues de Llobregat allowed the tax fraud trial after accusing her of neglecting to pay 13.9 million euros ($14.5 million) in taxes.

Shakira asserts that she has already paid off all of her debts in full, along with 3 million euros ($2.8 million) in interest.
