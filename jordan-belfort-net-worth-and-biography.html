Early Life

Jordan Belfort was born in Queens, New York on the 9th of July, 1962. His parents, Max and Leah, are both accountants and are Jewish families. Belfort earned a biology bachelor’s degree from American University.

He enrolled in the University of Maryland School of Dentistry

The University of Maryland School of Dentistry was where he made the decision to enroll. He left his college on his first day after hearing the following from the dean:

“The golden age of dentistry is over. If you’re here simply because you’re looking to make a lot of money, you’re in the wrong place.”  During the summer between his senior year of high school and his freshman year of college, Belfort and a close friend made $20,000 selling Italian ice to beachgoers from coolers.

Belfort received a biology degree from American University. At the University of Maryland School, he registered for dental school. When a professor stated that becoming a dentist wasn’t a method to become wealthy after the first day of lectures, he departed.

Career

At an early age, he worked for a meat company, where he established the company sales record in his first week there, among other sales positions, in his early years.

When he was 25, he applied for bankruptcy and was hired as a trainee stockbroker at L.F. Rothschild. According to rumors, Belfort’s first supervisor advised him that hookups, cocaine, and masturbation were the secrets to success.

In the wake of the 1987 Black Monday stock market meltdown, he was fired from this company. Belfort was nevertheless obsessed with the concept of earning the same amount of money as the more experienced stockbrokers.

Belfort worked for several financial companies in the late 1980s, taking up all the information he could. He polished his sales technique before deciding to launch his own business in 1989.

In 1987, he chose to use his sales abilities in a different industry and became a stockbroker. In the 1990s, Belfort founded Stratton Oakmont, his own business. He gained notoriety for defrauding investors of millions of dollars.

Jordan used a “pump and dump” strategy with his coworker Danny Porush to launder money. The prices of the stocks were inflated by his brokers, and Stratton Oakmont afterward sold its own shares in these stocks for a profit.

Belfort led a lavish lifestyle because of his wealth. He purchased a home, posh gadgets, and sports automobiles. He began to acquire a terrible drug addiction. He had a history of mishaps, including one in which he crashed his helicopter into his own yacht. Oh by God, what a life!

More so, his second marriage was made more difficult by his drug use. Belfort began acting carelessly with his employees. In Stratton Oakmont’s offices, sex, drug usage, and horseplay were commonplace.

In 2003, Belfort was given a four-year prison term and a $110 million fine. He was imprisoned for 22 months. Belfort was inspired to write about his experiences by one of his cellmates. He wrote his memoir, The Wolf of Wall Street, after spending his entire life in prison.

Catching The Wolf of Wall Street, which described his life after prison, was published soon after his first book. His third book, Way of the Wolf, was published in 2017. Today, Belfort travels the globe as a motivational speaker, sharing his story of bittersweet triumph. And teaching marketing techniques and providing sales training to businesses.
