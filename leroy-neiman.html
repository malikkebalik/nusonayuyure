Biography

Leroy Neiman was born in Saint Paul, MN on June 8, 1921. An expressionist painter is known for his brightly colored screen prints of musicians and athletes. He worked for Playboy Magazine for five decades, creating the Femlin character on the Party Jokes page. He met Hugh Hefner when they both worked for the Carson Pirie Scott department store chain. He served in the United States Army during World War II, where he frequently painted sets for Red Cross performances. He used the G.I. bill to attend the School of The Art Institute of Chicago. He was hired by Playboy in 1954.
