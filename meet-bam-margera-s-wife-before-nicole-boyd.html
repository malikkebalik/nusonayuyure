Missy Rothstein: Early Life and Biography

Melissa “Missy” is an American actress who was born on June 3, 1980, in Springfield, Pennsylvania. She was raised by a single mother, Marian Rothstein, with whom she spent the better part of her childhood.

Missy attended West Chester East, and upon graduation, she gained admission to the Pennsylvania State University. She graduated with a degree in Communications.

She Had a Career in Showbiz

Right from when she was a child, the American actress always wanted to be in the spotlight. She had a passion for modeling and even ditched her degree to pursue modeling. However, fate had other plans and she ended up becoming an actress.

Her first ever acting role was in 2003, when she got a role in the comedy romance movie, Haggard, which was written by her ex-husband, Bam Margera, who also starred in it. 2 years later, she made her debut in the reality TV series, Viva La Bam and Bam’s Unholy Union.

Missy Rothstein Met Her Ex-Husband Since High School

Bam Margera whose real name is Brandon Cole started off as Missy’s friend while they were both in high school. The duo attended West Chester High School. At the time, they were just good friends before their relationship turned romantic in 2003, years after they graduated.

Before Bam became famous for being a stuntman, he would shoot videos of himself and his friends skateboarding while doing crazy stunts. After the duo reconnected in 2003 and started dating, the relationship spiraled and in 2006, Bam proposed to Missy in front of a Cartier store. He revealed that he saw a diamond ring that fits perfectly on her finger, which he thought was a good sign.

They officially got married on February 3, 2007, at the Loews Hotel, in downtown Philadelphia, which aired on MTV’s Bam Unholy Union. Missy Got Divorced From The American Stuntman in 2012

In 2010, Missy’s marriage to Bam started experiencing challenges which led to the deterioration of their marriage. The major reason for their problem was Bam’s late-night partying in the house and his excessive drinking.

One time the reality TV star had to call 911 for help after Bam had been drinking for 4 days in a row. Eventually, the skateboarder asked her to move out of the house because of the constant fights and disagreements. They eventually got divorced in 2012.

After the divorce was finalized, Missy celebrated and tweeted that she was finally free, and asked her fans to join her in the celebration.
